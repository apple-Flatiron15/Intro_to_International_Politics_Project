Federations

A Federation is a nation with its overall power divided between the federal (central) government and its respective [mostly] self-governing states/regions/provinces that could mirror (to a certain extent) their federal counterpart in terms of adminstrative, legislative, and judicial power. In most cases, the constitution of a federation would lay out, in general, the powers, responsibilities, and rights of its political divisions (differs between countries).

These political divisions (e.g., states, regions, provinces (names vary between different federations)) would also have their own constitutions (among other important documents that stress their own obligations and commitments to their own citizens, along with their relationships with their federal counterparts (e.g., criminal codes, human rights acts) and other jurisdictions).
